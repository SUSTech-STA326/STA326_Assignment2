Data Description

We have two data sets: data_orders and data_offers, both being stored in a CSV format. The data_orders data set contains the following columns:

  • order_datetime - time of the order
  • origin_longitude - longitude of the order
  • origin_latitude - latitude of the order
  • m_order_eta - time before order arrival
  • order_gk - order number
  • order_status_key - status, an enumeration consisting of the following mapping:
    • 4 - cancelled by client,
    • 9 - cancelled by system, i.e., a reject
  • is_driver_assigned_key - whether a driver has been assigned
  • cancellation_time_in_seconds - how many seconds passed before cancellation

The data_offers data set is a simple map with 2 columns:

  • order_gk - order number, associated with the same column from the orders data set`
  • offer_id - ID of an offer

Analysis of Cancellation Trends by Hour

Overview

This analysis examines both the total volume and proportion of failed orders by hour, categorized into cancellations before driver assignment, after driver assignment, and system rejections.

Key Findings

Total Volumes and Proportional Breakdown

  • Peak Volumes:
    • The highest volume of cancellations occurs at 8:00 AM and 23:00 PM, with totals of 4110 and 2765 cancellations, respectively. These peaks suggest critical hours where operational interventions could be most impactful.
  • High Proportional Cancellations Before Assignment:
    • Dominant from 21:00 to 5:00, peaking at 88.48% at 21:00. This indicates potential inefficiencies in matching during hours of low driver availability or high passenger expectations.
  • Significant Cancellations After Assignment:
    • Elevated during evening rush hours (15:00 to 20:00), peaking at 42.56% at 19:00. Likely driven by traffic congestion and driver preferences affecting operational efficiency.

Trends Analysis

  • Morning and Evening Rush:
    • Both show increased cancellations after assignment, aligning with the start and end of typical work hours, thereby implicating external factors like traffic and shift timings in influencing cancellations.
  • Late Night:
    • Shows an unusually high rate of cancellations before assignment, potentially linked to reduced public transport availability or safety concerns.

Explanations

  • Demand-Supply Mismatch: Notably during rush hours, where demand spikes are not matched by driver availability, leading to higher post-assignment cancellations.
  • Passenger Decisions: Influenced by wait times and alternative transport options, particularly evident during late-night hours.
